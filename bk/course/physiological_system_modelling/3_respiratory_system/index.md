# 6 Airway Resistance

$$ R \propto \frac{L\eta}{r^4}$$

## 6.1 Parameters

- $L$: length of system
- $\eta$: viscousity of fluid flowing through
- $r$: radius of tubes in the system

## 6.2 Factors

- mucus accumulation from allergies or infections can dramatically increas resistance
- nearly 90% or airway resistance can normally be attributed to trachea and bronchi
- bronchioles are collapsible tubes, a decrease in their diameter can suddenly turn them into a significant source of airway resistance
  - asthma is an obstructive lung disease characterised by bronchoconstriction and airway edema

## 6.3 Inertance of System

Inertance of system also affects breathing

$$ P(t) = I\frac{dQ(t)}{dt}$$

# 8 Respiratory Oscillation Mechanics

Forced Oscillation Technique (FOT) explores the mechanical properties of respiratory system

<div class="wp-block-image">
  <figure class="aligncenter size-large"><a href="https://i1.wp.com/ppeetteerrsx.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/1-34.png?ssl=1"><img loading="lazy" width="274" height="258" src="https://i1.wp.com/ppeetteerrsx.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/1-34.png?resize=274%2C258&#038;ssl=1" alt="" class="wp-image-1408" data-recalc-dims="1" /></a><figcaption>Forced Oscillation Technique</figcaption></figure>
</div>

- Non-invasice
- Superimposing high-frequency small amplitude pressure oscillation at entrance of airway
- Computing the mechanical impedance Z of the respiratory system from flow-pressure relationship
- Replaces airway opening to room with a mouthpiece connected to a pressure source such as a loudspeaker
- Pressure source delivers sinusoidal pressure oscillations at one frequency each time - net pressure across lungs and chest is measured as input signal
- Resulting flow (output signal) gives the gain and phase shift
