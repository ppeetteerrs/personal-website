The urinary system (or excretory system) is the organ system that **produces, stores and eliminates urine**. It consists of the kidneys and the urinary tract. The kidneys make the urine, which is collected in the kidney pelvis. The urinary tract takes care of further excretion. The urinary tract contains both ureters, the bladder and the urethra. **Renal physiology** studies the **kidneys' function**.

**Position**

- **Left Kidney:** T12 to L3 vertebrae
- **Right Kidney:** lower due to slight displacement by the liver
- Upper portions of the kidneys are somewhat protected by the eleventh and twelfth ribs

**Dimensions**

- Each kidney weighs about 125–175 g in males and 115–155 g in females
- 11–14 cm in length, 6 cm wide, and 4 cm thick

**Outer Structures**

- **Fibrous Capsule:** dense, irregular connective tissue that directly covers the kidney, helps to hold its shape and provide protection
- **Renal Fat Pad**: shock-absorbing layer of adipose tissue covering the capsule
- **Fascia:** a tough layer which, together with the overlying peritoneum, serve to firmly anchor the kidneys to the posterior abdominal wall in a retroperitoneal position
- **Adrenal Gland:** small glands located on top of each kidney, directly influences renal function through production of the hormone aldosterone to stimulate sodium re-absorption

</div>

- **Renal Cortex:** outer region of kidney
- **Medulla:** inner region of kidney
- **Renal Columns:** connective tissue extensions that radiate downward from the cortex through the medulla to separate the most characteristic features of the medulla, the renal pyramids and the renal papillae. Also divides kidney into 6-8 lobes and provide a supportive framework for vessels that enter and exit the cortex
- **Renal Papillae:** bundles of collecting ducts that transport urine made by nephrons to the calyxes of the kidney for excretion
- **Renal Hilum:** entry and exit site for structures servicing the kidneys (vessels, nerves, lymphatics, ureters)
  - **Renal Pelvis:** emerges from the hilum, formed from the major and minor calyxes in the kidney. smooth muscle in the renal pelvis funnels urine via preistalsis into the ureter
  - **Renal Arteries:** formed directly from the descending aorta
  - **Renal Veins:** return cleansed blood directly to the inferior vena cava

Nephrons are the functional units of the kidney which cleanse the blood and balance the constituents of the circulation.

- **Glomerulues:** tuft of high-pressure capillaries about 200µm in diameter formed from the afferent arterioles
- **Bowman's Capsule:** a continuous sophisticated tubule whose proximal end surrounds the glomerulus in an intimate embrace

The kidney has two layers, composed by functional units called nephrons.

- Outer cortex: cortical nephrons
- Inner medulla: juxtamedullary nephrons

# 3 Renal System Analysis and Modelling

- Impulse Retention Response (assume LTI)
  - $C\_{kidney}=F\int\_0^tC_{in}(T)R(t-T)dT$
    - $C$: input
    - $C_{kidney}$: residual contents
    - $R(t)$is the impulse retention response
    - $F$ is the renal blood flow
